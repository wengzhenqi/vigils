# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — maintainers and contributors — pledge to make participation in the Vigils project a
harassment-free, respectful experience for everyone, regardless of background or identity.

This is a small open-source project maintained by volunteers. Good-faith, on-topic, civil
participation is welcome and appreciated. Bad-faith behaviour is not.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and constructive. Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Stay on topic. Use the right channel: **Issues** for actionable, reproducible bugs and concrete
  feature requests; **Discussions** for questions, help, and ideas; the **Security Policy** for
  vulnerabilities.
- Assume good faith and provide the information the issue templates ask for.

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following will be removed, closed, and/or reported to GitHub, and may result in a block:

- Harassment, insults, personal attacks, defamation, or trolling — including unfounded public
  accusations against the project or its maintainers (e.g. baseless "fake star" / fraud claims).
- Spam, advertising, promotion, link farming, or off-topic content.
- Low-effort, automated, or duplicate issues that ignore the templates.
- Sustained disruption of issues, pull requests, or discussions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit,
lock, or close any contribution or issue that violates this Code of Conduct, and may block the
account, without owing the offender a debate.

To report abuse:

1. **Report it to GitHub directly** — use the "Report" option on the issue, comment, or user
   profile (this is the fastest path; GitHub Trust & Safety removes spam/abuse accounts quickly).
2. You may also notify the maintainers privately via a
   [GitHub private security advisory](https://github.com/duncatzat/vigils/security/advisories/new),
   which reaches the maintainers without exposing the report publicly.

Enforcement decisions are at the maintainers' discretion and prioritise the safety and morale of
good-faith contributors over engagement with bad-faith actors.
